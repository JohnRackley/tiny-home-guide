10 Steps to Building a Tiny Home
in Southern Greenville County

Prepared as a helpful overview for buyers considering land in southern Greenville County, including Fountain Inn, Pelzer, and surrounding areas.

Building a small or tiny home can be a great option, but in the Upstate of South Carolina, success often depends on understanding zoning, land suitability, and site requirements before purchasing property.

10 Steps to Building a Tiny Home

  1. Define what “tiny” means under local zoning and building codes.
  2. Confirm zoning regulations before purchasing land.
  3. Understand septic requirements early in the process.
  4. Choose land with legal road frontage and access.
  5. Confirm availability of utilities (power, water, internet).
  6. Decide between a permanent foundation or a movable structure.
  7. Work with local professionals familiar with county requirements.
  8. Design for Upstate terrain, drainage, and soil conditions.
  9. Secure all required permits and approvals.
  10. Complete final inspections and prepare for move-in.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Assuming acreage automatically means buildable land.
  • Skipping zoning verification before making an offer.
  • Underestimating septic and site preparation costs.
  • Buying property without confirmed road access.
  • Assuming tiny homes on wheels are allowed everywhere.
  • Overlooking minimum square footage requirements.
  • Ignoring slope, drainage, and soil quality.
  • Failing to plan for utility connections.
  • Using builders unfamiliar with local codes.
  • Rushing decisions without understanding long-term implications.
